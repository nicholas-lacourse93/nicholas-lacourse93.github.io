My research studies behavioral economics with a focus on costly decision-making under uncertainty. I explore questions related to how monetary, cognitive, and physiological costs affect judgements about ourselves and others when the truth is not necessarily known. My work utilizes a variety of methods, but most utilize artefactual field experiments (online experiments) using representative sampling from either MTurk or Prolific. My work can broadly apply to fields such as industrial oraganization and job-search with a emphasis on information acquisition along with discrimination.

In my free time you will find me playing video games, engaging in web development, being an advocate for open science, and looking for creative ways to engage in more liberal forms of education.
